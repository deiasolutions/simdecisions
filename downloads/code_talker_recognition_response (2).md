## 1. Fairness

The proposed system is **well-structured and multidimensional**, recognizing contributions across technical, collaborative, and procedural domains. It supports diversity in contribution types, ensuring recognition extends beyond pure code output.

### Strengths
- Multiple recognition categories promote inclusivity of skill sets.
- Encourages both technical excellence and community engagement.
- Mix of automated (tokens, leaderboard) and human (peer recognition) mechanisms.

### Risks & Considerations
- **Subjectivity**: Token awards based on perceived impact may favor visible or vocal contributors.
- **Visibility bias**: Maintenance or refactor work may be undervalued.
- **Peer bias**: Popularity effects in voting can skew fairness.

### Recommendations
- Define **clear rubrics** for each category with measurable indicators (e.g., PR complexity scoring).  
- **Rotate evaluators** periodically to minimize entrenched bias.  
- Use **anonymous peer nominations** and allow written rationales for transparency.
